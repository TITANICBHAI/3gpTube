**Option B cannot work unless you *manufacture* black bars inside the video itself.**
Right now those black regions exist only in your *player*, not in the encoded frame.
FFmpeg cannot draw subtitles in a space that does not exist inside the video bitmap.

But you *can* simulate Option B by **adding internal black bars inside the 176×144 frame** and shifting the video upward or downward.
This keeps the final output resolution **exactly 176×144** and keeps subtitles *off your real content*.

This is the right engineering workaround.

# **Solution: Create artificial black bars *inside* 176×144**

You must shrink the actual *video area slightly* inside the 176×144 frame and fill the remaining space with black.
Then put subtitles into that black region.

You asked: *“Option B but away from real content”* → This is exactly that.

---

# **Variant 1: Put subtitles BELOW the video (bottom bar inside 176×144)**

### FFmpeg filter

```
-vf "scale=176:120:force_original_aspect_ratio=decrease,\
pad=176:144:(ow-iw)/2:0,setsar=1"
```

Explanation:

* Video scales to **176×120** (slightly smaller vertically, still proportional)
* Extra **24 px** at bottom becomes a built-in black bar
* Subtitles appear only in that bar
* Video stays undistorted
* Output stays 176×144

Then burn subtitles normally. They will sit in the 24px black bar.

---

# **Variant 2: Put subtitles ABOVE the video**

```
-vf "scale=176:120:force_original_aspect_ratio=decrease,\
pad=176:144:(ow-iw)/2:(144-ih),setsar=1"
```

Explanation:

* 24 px black bar added **above**
* Subtitles draw there

---

# **Variant 3: Create both top + bottom bars (cinematic) but video still centered**

```
-vf "scale=176:112:force_original_aspect_ratio=decrease,\
pad=176:144:(ow-iw)/2:(oh-ih)/2,setsar=1"
```

* Video shrinks to **112px** height
* 32px total free space (16px top + 16px bottom)
* You can assign subtitles to the bottom only

---

# **Bottom Line**

To place subtitles “away from real content” **inside the same 176×144 output**, you must:

1. Slightly reduce video height inside 176×144
2. Use the freed space (black bar) for subtitles
3. Keep aspect ratio intact so nothing looks stretched

This is the only valid Option B implementation.
